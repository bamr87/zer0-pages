# ESG Investing

## Definition

ESG — **Environmental, Social, and Governance** — is the framework for evaluating companies and investments on non-financial outcomes alongside financial ones. [[wiki/sources/wizard-topples-capitalist-dominance|The wizard post]] uses "ESG scorecard" as one of the three outputs of its audit engine and points to "the rise of fintech regulation, ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) investing, and ethical AI frameworks" as evidence that "the real-world movement toward accountable, equitable technology is well underway."

## How It Works

The post's stance is that ESG is only as credible as its measurement, and measurement is an engineering problem:

- **Environmental & Social → numbers** via [[wiki/concepts/impact-weighted-accounting|impact-weighted accounting]] (the `impact-weights.yml` schema, with negative weights for emissions and water intensity).
- **Governance → audits** via [[wiki/concepts/algorithmic-accountability|algorithmic accountability]] reports and [[wiki/concepts/stakeholder-capitalism|stakeholder-first]] board structures.
- **Integrated reporting** — impact metrics belong "on the same dashboard as financial metrics — not a separate 'ESG appendix.'"

## Why It Matters

ESG is where the post answers its own hardest question — "Isn't 'shareholder value plus ethics' a contradiction?" — with **"Not necessarily."** It cites [[wiki/entities/b-corp|B Corp certification]] and the Embankment Project for Inclusive Capitalism (EPIC) as demonstrations that "long-horizon shareholder returns are *positively* correlated with stakeholder welfare." The apparent contradiction, it argues, "only appears under quarterly-earnings tunnel vision."

## Examples

- **ESG scorecard branch** — the audit engine emits an ESG scorecard directly from the open ledger (see [[wiki/concepts/radical-transparency|the transparency pipeline]]).
- **Correlation evidence** — B Corp and EPIC as named real-world proofs that returns and welfare can align over long horizons.
