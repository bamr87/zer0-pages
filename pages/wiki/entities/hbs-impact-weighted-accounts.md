# HBS Impact-Weighted Accounts Project

## Overview

The **Impact-Weighted Accounts Project** is a Harvard Business School research initiative to create financial statements that reflect a company's social and environmental impacts, not just its monetary performance. In [[wiki/sources/wizard-topples-capitalist-dominance|the wizard post]] it is the authoritative real-world reference behind [[wiki/concepts/impact-weighted-accounting|impact-weighted accounting]] — the citation that grounds the post's declarative `impact-weights.yml` example in an established methodology. It appears in the post's Further Reading as the [HBS Impact-Weighted Accounts Project](https://www.hbs.edu/impact-weighted-accounts/).

## Key Facts

- **Purpose:** develop a standardized way to monetize and incorporate impacts (environmental, employment, product) into accounts so they can be compared alongside financial line items.
- **Why the post cites it:** it moves impact-weighting from "nice idea" to a named, citable framework — reinforcing the checklist demand that impact metrics live "on the same dashboard as financial metrics — not a separate 'ESG appendix.'"
- **Positioning:** an academic anchor for the broader [[wiki/concepts/esg-investing|ESG]] movement the post points to.
